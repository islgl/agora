The Mark · A3 · Final

For ἀγορά

A compass of eight olive leaves — four cardinal, four diagonal — around a wine-dark point. A sign for the reading mind: radiant, rooted, pointed in every direction at once.

Construction

01

Eight leaves rotate around a shared axis. The four long leaves sit on the cardinal axes (N, E, S, W); the four short leaves bisect them at 45°. Each leaf is an almond curve drawn from the center out, its inner edge stopping 14 units short of origin — opening a small breath around the wine-dark dot. The dot is --agora-wine, the one accent reserved for punctuation and the letter's pulse.

Variants

02

The two-color canonical is the default everywhere there's room for it. Mono is for when color would be noise — embossing, small UI, single channel printing. On dark grounds, the dot shifts from --agora-wine to its lamplit counterpart --agora-wine dark-mode token (#c08b97), which is the system's built-in adaptation.

Scale

03

At 16 px the wine dot disappears — it has become noise at that resolution, and the favicon SVG omits it. From 24 px upward, the dot returns. Below 12 px, prefer the mono mark or the wordmark.

Wordmark Lockup

04

The mark sits to the left of the wordmark, separated by a single hairline rule at --agora-rule. The mark's height matches the x-height of the wordmark plus its cap-height — in practice, scale the mark to 48% of the wordmark's type size.
